About Me

Hello, my name is Graham, and hopefully if you're reading this you are considering my application for Founders and Coders. Before I tell you about why I'm applying, I'd like to just tell you a bit about myself...

I am 28, originally from Reading but have lived in London for roughly two and a half years. When I was 22, i went to University to study Music. My interests are music, cooking, cycling and meeting up with friends (back when that was allowed).

I've always loved technology and music, which is why for this website I decided to build a synthesiser. Please check it out before you go, I spent ages on it.

Why I'm Applying

In short: Because I want to become a web developer, and because Founders and Coders sounds like a great place to do that. A friend of mine did the course quite a few years ago, and speaking to them has made me really admire the work of the organisation.

I grew up with friends from a diverse range of backgrounds, but then going to University to study music, and having worked in the arts sector for the last few years, I am aware how my privilege has enabled me to do this. I;ve also seen how disproportionately people with my same background are represented in these areas, and the detrimental effects that has. I believe the internet is the greatest resource we have for changing this, because access to the internet can break down so many of the barriers to information, experience and knowledge.

I've always been a geek, and I've always spent ages on computers, mostly making or listening to music. From listening to electronic music, I've gotten into synthesisers, and from there I've gotten into programming using Max MSP. I've also played around with trying to make music in Unity, so have learnt some bits of C#. Through doing this, I've found that I really enjoy sitting down and solving technical problems, which is how I approached building this synthesiser. Once I found out about the WebAudio API, I had to think about what I wanted to make, and then solve every problem to get there.

My interest in programming has been steadily growing over the last few years, but since the pandemic hit I have found out about an open-source programme called VCV Rack, and I think it's the coolest thing ever. It's basically a software recreation of lots of modular synthesisers, and there is a whole community of people who contribute their own designs for free. There is also a tonne of people doing free tutorials on Youtube. It's written in C++, and it's not hosted on the web, but I think that it's amazing that with an internet connection and a free piece of software you can synthesise sounds that would previously need thousands of pounds worth of hardware. The reason why I'm talking about this is because finding out about that software, and listening to interviews of its creator, has made me decide to switch lanes and commit to trying to become a web developer. Although there's issues around artists getting paid, I think it's great how things like mixcloud, soundcloud, spotify, shazam and fiverr have opened up music. And how right now I'm typing this into google docs without having to pay for microsoft word. I'm not sure exactly what I want to do for work, but I'd love to work somewhere that opens up doors to cool things, and I think being a web developer is the best way of doing that.

I'm a sociable person, and I think I'm pretty open-minded and comfortable in the company of all sorts of people. I think that it is much easier to learn and solve problems as a team, and if i were to be offered a place on the course, I would be hoping to make a lot of new friends and work with all of the people on the course to succeed together. Through making music, I've worked with loads of people on loads of different collaborative projects and really enjoyed it, and it would be great to be able to bring that spirit to Founders and Coders.

Thanks for reading, I hope I didn't go on too long.
